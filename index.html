About Me

I am a senior at Stanford studying engineering-physics and pursuing a masters in computer science.

In my course work I have worked on a variety of projects including building a 2D plotter drawing robot on a bare metal Raspberry Pi, applying transfer learning to retrain a 3D convolutional neural network model to identify American sign language words, using neural nets to emulate the Rubin Observatory’s deblending pipeline, and training a machine vision model on synthetic images created in Blender.

For my professional background, I have now worked as a software developer at three different companies. In the winter of 2021, I worked for StoneAge, a robotics waterblast company, doing full stack development with Node.js/React and the AWS IoT core. In the summer of 2021, I worked for Boecore on a Missile Defense Agency project as a modeling and simulation intern developing applications in C++ and Java with an emphasis on Object Oriented Programming and Test-Driven Development. This most recent summer, I worked at SpaceX on the Starlink Fleet Software Team.

In my free time, I enjoy changing my altitude whenever possible: climbing, cycling, and trail running.

Classical Machine Vision Satellite Orientation Algorithm

The goal was to identify the relative position and orientation of Satellite A based on a picture from Satellite B. In order to generate test data, I used the 3D modeling software Blender to create photorelistic images of a cubsate with Leds on the corners. I then wrote a classical machine vision algorithm using openCV to crop in on the Satellite and apply a PNP algorithm to identify the relative position and rotation.
